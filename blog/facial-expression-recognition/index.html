Facial Expression Recognition

By Golnar Mahani August 04, 2017 August 07, 2017

In this post the research paper of L Ma. and K. Khorasani titled, “Facial Expression Recognition Using Constructive Feedforward Neural Networks” has been reviewed.

In this paper, a method has been proposed to detect four different facial expressions including smile, anger, sadness, and surprise. The feature extraction is done based on a 2-D discrete cosine transformation, and a constructive feed-forward neural network (CFFNN) with one-hidden-layer is used as the classifier.

Motivation

The classification methods based on neural networks (NN) had promising results in previous researches. So in this paper, the facial expressions are classified using NN. Since defining an appropriate size for NN is time consuming and was done experimentally in previous researches, in this paper a CFFNN method is used. This method is more efficacious, in terms of training computational costs, than other back-propagation and radial basis function methods.

CFFNN Advantages

In CFFNN, the structure of the neural network is constructed step by step through the process of learning. In this approach, the process is initialized with a small neural network and when required, hidden units or hidden layers are added to the network. The process continues until no improvement occurs or the desired accuracy is achieved. One of the advantages of CFFNN over the pruning algorithms is that a CFFNN is initialized with a small network, but pruning algorithms start with a network for which determining the right size is a challenge. Furthermore, as mentioned before, the CFFNN is beneficial in terms of computational costs, because the size of network increments depending on how the problem is complex. In contrast with pruning and regularization-based algorithms that require specifying particular parameters depending on the problem, CFFNN is not constrained with such limitations that affect the performance of the network.

The application of CFFNN to facial expression recognition

The first phase in this research for building the model is the preprocessing phase. In this phase, the relevant features should be extracted from the images. For this purpose, first the difference between the neutral image and each of the facial expression images is calculated to have features that are independent of the appearance of individuals. Second, the dimension of the obtained features should be reduced. So, a 2-D discrete cosine transformation (DCT) is applied to the data. This technique transforms the image into the frequency domain. After the transformation is done, a block from the lower frequency 2-D DCT coefficients, which are claimed to contain the most relevant information of the image, is selected as the key input features for the neural network.
In this paper, a one-hidden-layer NN is constructed. In order to have a smaller network a pruning strategy is used locally, and the connections that do not significantly help to maximize the objective function or to reduce the training error, are identified and removed. The proposed method is developed and evaluated by the normalized images of 60 men. The model is compared to the Vector Matching and Fixed-size NN methods proposed by Y. Inada et al and Y. Xiao et al, respectively. In average, the results of the CFFNN system outperformed these two methods.

The results show that the “sadness” and “anger” expressions are the challenging expressions for the CFFNN recognition system. Moreover, although in the proposed method the neural network is constructed incrementally and the resulting network is suboptimal, the high performance of the model is highly dependent on properly identifying the size of the 2-D DCT coefficient blocks.

References
